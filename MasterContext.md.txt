## WHAT THIS PROJECT IS

**Privacy Lens** — A Universal Privacy Policy Translator.

An AI-powered web application that:
- Accepts any privacy policy (paste text or URL)
- Segments it into individual clauses
- Classifies each clause into Red (high risk) / Green (protective) / Gray (ambiguous)
- Detects vagueness in deliberately obscure language
- Flags rights that are ABSENT from the policy
- Generates plain-English explanations per clause
- Produces a 0–100 risk score
- Outputs structured 4-question format:
  1. What data is collected?
  2. Who is it shared with?
  3. What are your rights?
  4. What should you watch out for?

---

## RESEARCH ANGLE

**Primary claim:** First DPDPA 2023 aligned privacy policy classifier trained 
on Indian company policy data.

**Three novel contributions:**
1. First DPDPA-annotated clause-level corpus of Indian privacy policies
2. Vagueness detection — quantifies deliberate linguistic obfuscation (novel metric)
3. Absence detection — flags user rights missing from policy (no existing system does this)

**Closest existing work:** Polisis (USENIX 2018, CMU) — English only, 
no DPDPA alignment, no vagueness detection, no absence detection, 
no plain-English explanations for users.

**Target venues:** FIRE 2025, LREC-COLING (resource track), arXiv

## KNOWN ISSUES AND DECISIONS MADE

### Issues:
1. **Too many Gray clauses** (~83%) — Root cause: SVM misclassifies 
   category → wrong keyword set checked → defaults to Gray. 
   Fix: BERT fine-tuning on DPDPA data will reduce this significantly.

2. **Vagueness detection** works but many clauses score Gray because 
   they use unusual language not in keyword lists. This is expected 
   and honest — the system does not fabricate classifications.

3. **Gray clauses are real** — Many policy clauses genuinely are neutral 
   boilerplate. 40-45% Gray after BERT is realistic and accurate.

### Decisions Made:
- Removed all Gemini API calls — switched to local spaCy simplification
- No clause limit — all clauses in policy are analyzed
- simplify_clause uses entity extraction → specific explanation OR 
  honest fallback → never wrong/fabricated explanation
- sys.path.insert fix applied to pipeline.py and frontend/app.py
- Windows: use venv\Scripts\activate
